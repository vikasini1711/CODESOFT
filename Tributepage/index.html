A Tribute to Mother Teresa

Saint of the Gutters | A Symbol of Selfless Love

About Mother Teresa

Mother Teresa, born in 1910 in Skopje, was a Catholic nun and missionary who dedicated her life to serving the poor and sick in India. She founded the Missionaries of Charity in Kolkata and became a global icon of compassion and humanitarian service.

Admirable Qualities

  • Compassionate:
    Mother Teresa's unwavering compassion for the marginalized, particularly the poorest of the poor, is a defining characteristic of her life and legacy. She demonstrated compassion through her actions, dedicating her life to serving the needy, sick, and dying with love and dignity. She founded the Missionaries of Charity, an organization that continues to provide care and support for those in need around the world.
    1.Serving the Downtrodden:
    Mother Teresa's work in Calcutta, India, focused on serving the marginalized and the suffering, including those living in slums, facing poverty, and struggling with disease.
    2.Founding the Missionaries of Charity:
    She established the Missionaries of Charity, an organization that provides care and support to the poor, abandoned, and vulnerable, including those living on the streets, suffering from diseases like HIV/AIDS, and those in leper colonies.
    3.Treating Everyone with Dignity:
    Regardless of their religious beliefs or social status, Mother Teresa treated everyone with compassion and respect, offering love and support to all.
  • Humble:
    Mother Teresa's humility is a cornerstone of her teachings and actions. She believed in living a life of selfless service, recognizing that true greatness lies not in personal achievements but in serving others and relying on God's guidance. This humble approach allowed her to connect with the marginalized and vulnerable, fostering a deep sense of compassion and understanding.
    4.Recognizing Dependence on God:
    Mother Teresa understood that her actions were only possible through God's grace, and she saw herself as an instrument of His love and service.
    5.Living Among Those Served:
    She encouraged her sisters to live alongside the poor, experiencing their struggles firsthand and fostering empathy.
  • Fearless:
    Mother Teresa's fearless actions stemmed from her deep-seated compassion and belief in serving the poorest of the poor. She faced challenges with courage and determination, establishing the Missionaries of Charity to provide care, love, and support to those in need, including those living on the streets, abandoned children, and lepers.
    1.Establishing the Missionaries of Charity:
    In 1950, she bravely founded the Missionaries of Charity in Kolkata, India, a time when few organizations focused on the poorest of the poor.
    2.Working in underserved communities:
    She established homes for the dying, hospices for lepers, and children's homes, defying societal norms and stereotypes about these marginalized groups.
    3.Challenging the powerful:
    She was not afraid to challenge the powerful, including political leaders, to advocate for the plight of the poor and suffering, even at the cost of personal risks.
    4.Adapting to new situations:
    She was willing to adapt her methods and work in different countries and cultures, always prioritizing the needs of the poor and those who were most vulnerable.
    5.Maintaining her faith and resilience:
    Even in the face of criticism and personal struggles, Mother Teresa remained committed to her faith and continued to serve tirelessly. These actions demonstrated Mother Teresa's unwavering commitment to serving humanity and her courage in the face of adversity.
  • Inspiring:
    Mother Teresa's inspiration stemmed from her profound religious faith, a strong sense of compassion, and a personal call to serve the poorest of the poor. She was deeply influenced by the lives of saints and missionaries, particularly those dedicated to helping the sick and needy. A pivotal moment came during a train journey when she felt a "call within a call" to leave her teaching position and work directly with the impoverished communities in Kolkata.
    1. Religious Faith:
    Her Catholic faith was a cornerstone of her life, shaping her values and guiding her actions.
    She was inspired by the lives of saints and missionaries who devoted themselves to serving others.
    She saw Jesus in the suffering of the poor, fueling her desire to alleviate their suffering.
    She was particularly devoted to the Immaculate Heart of Mary, choosing it as her congregation's patroness.
    2. Compassion and Empathy:
    Mother Teresa was deeply moved by the poverty and suffering she witnessed in Kolkata. She felt a strong sense of empathy for the sick, the dying, the hungry, and the homeless. Her compassion led her to dedicate her life to serving those in need.
    3. Personal Call to Action:
    During a train journey, she experienced a powerful moment that solidified her calling to work with the poorest of the poor. She heard Jesus telling her to leave her teachings and live amongst the poor, serving them directly. This experience led her to give up her possessions and live in poverty with the people she sought to help.
    4. Influence of Others:
    Her mother, a devout Catholic, instilled in her a love for God and a desire to serve others. She was also inspired by the lives of missionaries and saints who had dedicated themselves to serving the poor and sick.
    5. The Missionaries of Charity:
    In 1950, she founded the Missionaries of Charity, an organization dedicated to serving the poor and sick. The organization has grown to serve around the world, offering love and care to those who cannot afford it. Her work continues to inspire people to serve others and make a difference in the lives of the poor.
"Not all of us can do great things. But we can do small things with great love." – Mother Teresa
